# RecipeMise / Mise Competitive Analysis
### PrepTable vs. Mise (RecipeMise) -- The Namesake Competitor

**Research date:** July 6, 2026
**Source analysis:** recipemise.com, Apple App Store, Google Play Store

**Name collision:** The product is called "Mise" (short for "mise en place"), the developer is "Recipe Mise LLC," the domain is recipemise.com. Same culinary term as "PrepTable." This is the third app we've found playing on the same name (PrepTable, Meez, Mise).

---

## Executive Summary

1. **Mise (recipemise.com) is the most feature-complete competitor matching our vision.** It covers the entire workflow: AI recipe creation (from photo, link, or description), web + social import, weekly meal planning, smart shopping lists by aisle, hands-free voice-guided cooking, and prep scheduling. It's the closest apples-to-apples competitor we've found.
2. **It's very early stage.** 50+ Android downloads, too few iOS ratings to show a score. Launched around late May / early June 2026. Developer is Thomas Carlisle (sole operator?) in Pottsville, Pennsylvania under Recipe Mise LLC.
3. **It's native mobile** -- live on both iOS and Android app stores. We're a PWA. This is a distribution advantage for them.
4. **Premium is $4.99/mo or $49.99/yr.** AI recipe creation, social import, photo import, smart scaling, voice cooking, and translation are gated behind the paywall. Free tier covers basic recipe box and web import.
5. **The name collision is now a real problem.** Three apps named "Mise" or "Meez" or "PrepTable" in the same market. Users will confuse them. Search competition will be fierce.

## 4. Key Weaknesses (Our Opportunities)

### 1. Very Early Stage
50+ Android downloads, no ratings. One developer in Pennsylvania. This could be a side project that doesn't get sustained investment. The rapid version history (v1.6.8 to v1.10.1 in a month) suggests active development, but they're building from scratch.

**Opportunity:** We can move faster and win on feature depth while they're still finding product-market fit.

### 2. Premium Gates the Core Differentiators
AI recipe creation, photo import, social import, voice cooking, and translation are all Premium. The free tier is basically a recipe box with web import and basic planning. Users hit the paywall fast.

**Opportunity:** We have Claude-powered AI features available to all users. No gating.

### 3. No Recipe Ranking
No Elo system, no head-to-head comparison, no structured ranking at all. "Track what you've cooked" is basic checkmark tracking.

**Opportunity:** Elo ranking is unique and Mise doesn't have anything like it.

### 4. No Cooking History / Habit Loop
"Track what you've cooked" is mentioned but appears to be basic. No streak tracking, no detailed cook log, no frequency-based recommendations.

**Opportunity:** Our cooking history and habit features compound over time and create switching costs.

### 5. No Skill System
No techniques, no skill progression, no mastery tracking. Mise helps you cook individual recipes but doesn't help you become a better cook.

**Opportunity:** Our technique taxonomy and skill map (features 03-06) are completely unique in the competitive landscape.

### 6. No Household Model
Share cookbooks is mentioned but it's not clear if this is real-time household sync with separate preferences. Likely just share-a-cookbook.

**Opportunity:** Our household model (shared recipes, separate rankings) is a better fit for couples.

### 7. No Grocery Savings
No cost estimation, no sale matching, no budget tracking.

**Opportunity:** Feature 17 (grocery savings engine) addresses a need Mise doesn't touch.

### 8. Single Developer Risk
Thomas Carlisle is the sole developer per the Google Play listing. If he gets busy, burns out, or moves on, the app stalls. No team.

**Opportunity:** We're building with more resources and long-term commitment.

## 6. Strategy Implications

**This is the competitor that looks most like our roadmap.** They have: URL import, social import, AI recipe creation, shopping lists, meal planning, and voice-guided hands-free cooking. Everything we're planning to build, they've already shipped in some form.

**The key difference is depth vs. breadth.** Mise has a broad feature set at surface level. We have deeper features in fewer areas (ranking, cooking history, skill system, grocery consolidation). The question is whether we can fill our gaps (import, cook mode) before Mise deepens their foothold.

**The name confusion is a serious concern.** Three apps in the "mise en place" naming space:
1. **PrepTable** (our app) -- PWA, free, deeper features
2. **Meez** (meezenplace.app) -- web app, visual cook mode, early stage
3. **Mise** (recipemise.com) -- native mobile, broadest feature set, very early stage

This creates search confusion, app store confusion, and brand dilution. If Mise gets traction in the App Store before we have a native app, they capture users searching for "mise" or "mise en place recipe app."

**Urgent actions this suggests:**
1. **Ship URL import.** Mise and Meez both have it. Without it, we're not in the consideration set.
2. **Evaluate native mobile.** Mise is native on both platforms. Our PWA is functional but "PWA" is still a caveat in competitive comparisons.
3. **Consider brand differentiation.** If Mise and Meez both grow, our shared naming convention becomes a liability. Might be worth exploring clearer branding before the space gets more crowded.

**The wedge:** Depth over breadth. Mise has many features implemented at v1 quality. We have fewer features but each one is deeper and more polished. Ranking, cooking history, skill system, and grocery savings are features Mise doesn't have at any quality level. Win on those.
